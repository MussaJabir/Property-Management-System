Fixes & Improvements Report
Date: 11 June 2026
• Fixes & improvements

This report covers two changes: stronger control over removing a client and all their data, and a new guided welcome tour that helps first-time users find their way around.

In short: you can now permanently and completely remove a client when you really mean to — safely, with a confirmation step — and new landlords and renters are greeted by a short, skippable walkthrough the first time they sign in.

What we changed

1. Completely remove a client and all of their data Data control
What we found: when you deleted a client, the client disappeared from the list but their underlying records — properties, units, renters, leases, invoices, payments, files — were left behind. Deleting did not actually clear everything out.
  • Two clear steps now. “Delete” simply archives a client (they are hidden but fully recoverable). A separate “Purge permanently” action does the complete removal.
  • A safety check before anything is erased. To purge, you must type the client’s exact name to confirm — so a complete wipe can never happen by accident.
  • Purging clears everything that belongs to that client — all their properties, units, renters, leases, invoices, payments, receipts, expenses, website pages, staff accounts and every uploaded file.
  • Easier to find archived clients. The Clients screen now has Active / Archived / All tabs (with counts), so archived clients are one click away instead of hidden behind a filter.
What it means: you have full control to remove a client completely when you need to, with a strong safety net — archive first (reversible), purge only with a deliberate, typed confirmation. Nothing is left lingering behind the scenes.
2. A guided welcome tour for first-time users New feature
New landlords and renters are now welcomed by a short, animated, skippable walkthrough the first time they sign in. It points at each part of the screen and explains, step by step, what it is for — so people can get started without training.
  • For landlords, the tour follows the natural setup order: Locations → Properties → Units → Renters → Leases → Invoices & payments → Maintenance → Reports.
  • For renters, it covers their dashboard summary, invoices, maintenance requests, notifications, and profile / language settings.
  • Skip anytime, and it won’t keep reappearing once finished or skipped. A “Replay tour” option lets anyone go through it again whenever they like.
  • Available in English and Swahili, styled to match each dashboard, and it works on phones and computers alike.
What it means: new users understand the system faster and feel confident from day one, which reduces setup questions and support requests.

Quality & assurance
