Feature Update
Date: 2 June 2026
• Listings & operator experience

This update makes your listings stand out and your day-to-day admin faster. Every unit can now have its own set of photos and a clean public detail page; you can tag units with the features renters search for (AC, parking, water, security, and more); and the staff control panel has a tidier, more glanceable dashboard plus quicker navigation.

Everything below is bilingual (English & Kiswahili) and works on phones, tablets, and desktop, just like the rest of PMS.

What's new for your renters

1. Each unit can have its own photos
Previously every unit on your public website borrowed the building's photo. Now you can upload a dedicated set of photos for each individual unit — the living area, kitchen, bedroom, and bathroom — so renters see exactly what they would be renting.
  • Upload several photos per unit; drag to reorder; the first one is the cover.
  • If you don't upload any, the unit automatically falls back to the building's photos — so a listing is never blank.
2. A dedicated page for every unit
Clicking a unit now opens its own page with a photo gallery, key details (bedrooms, bathrooms, size), the rent, an "Enquire about this unit" button, and a few other available units to keep people browsing. Each unit page has its own shareable web link.
3. Amenities — the features renters care about
You can now tag each unit with the amenities it offers. They appear as neat tags on the listing and detail page, and renters can filter to show only units that have what they need.
  • Air conditioning, Wi-Fi, 24/7 water, backup power, parking, security, furnished, hot water, fitted kitchen, balcony, CCTV, en-suite bathroom, garden, servant's quarter.
  • All translated into Kiswahili automatically.

What's new for you & your staff

4. A clearer, more useful dashboard
The staff dashboard was reorganised so the numbers that matter are at the top, and you no longer have to scroll endlessly.
  • Four key figures at a glance: occupancy, money collected this month (with a 6-month trend line), outstanding, and overdue.
  • A bar chart of collections over the last 6 months and a doughnut chart of units by status, side by side.
  • The "who owes me" and "recent payments" lists now sit side by side instead of stacked.
  • The setup checklist quietly disappears once your workspace is up and running.
5. Faster everyday admin
  • Quick status tabs on Units (Vacant / Occupied / Maintenance), Invoices (Overdue / Unpaid / Paid / Draft), and Maintenance (Pending / In progress / Completed) — each with a live count, so you can jump straight to what needs attention.
  • After you add a property, unit, renter, invoice, or anything else, the system now takes you straight back to the list so you immediately see what you just added.
  • Unit photos now show as thumbnails in your unit list, and empty screens explain the next step for a brand-new workspace.
