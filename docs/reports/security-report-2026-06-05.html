Security & Access Control Report
Date: 5 June 2026
• Security hardening

We carried out a full security review of PMS and worked through a prioritised programme of fixes and improvements. This report summarises that work in plain language — what was found, what we changed, and what it means for you and your renters.

The headline: we closed a serious sign-in weakness in the renter portal, tightened how data is protected behind the scenes, blocked spam on public forms, and put landlords properly in control of their staff — who they are, what they can do, and how they get access. No passwords are emailed anywhere any more; everyone sets their own through a secure one-time link.

What we reviewed and fixed

1. Renter portal sign-in security Critical
What we found: renter portal accounts were created with a password derived from the renter's own phone number — which is also their username — with no limit on repeated guessing. In practice, anyone who knew a renter's phone number could have signed in and seen their invoices, payment history and personal details.
  • Renters now receive a secure, one-time activation link and choose their own private password. No password is ever generated or emailed.
  • Repeated failed sign-in attempts are now automatically blocked (rate-limiting), defeating password guessing.
  • Any pre-existing accounts using the old weak password were invalidated and must re-activate.
What it means: a renter's account can only be accessed by the renter, using a password only they know.
2. Cross-account data protection High
We hardened the system so that no submitted form or request can change which landlord a record belongs to. Every record (property, renter, lease, invoice, payment, and so on) is now locked to its owning workspace at the data layer, and an automatic test proves a tampered request cannot move data between landlords.
What it means: defence-in-depth — even a malformed or malicious request cannot misattribute one landlord's data to another.
3. Spam & abuse protection on public forms Medium
The public “Contact us” form on each landlord's website could be used to flood the system and spam landlords with messages. We added two protections: a cap on how many messages one visitor can send in a short window, and a hidden anti-bot trap.
What it means: genuine enquiries get through; automated spam is blocked before it reaches the landlord's inbox.
4. Access control & authorisation gaps Medium
We tightened who can do what across the system:
  • Renters can now only raise a maintenance request for a unit they actually rent (not any unit).
  • Access checks now “fail safe” — if anything is uncertain, access is denied rather than allowed.
  • We confirmed and locked, with automatic tests, the strict separation between BJP's control panel and each landlord's own panel.
5. Staff roles & permissions — landlords in control New capability
Previously every staff member a landlord added could do everything. Staff now have proper, enforced roles, and landlords manage their own team from two new screens (Team and Roles & Permissions):

  • Team: invite staff, set each person's role, and deactivate anyone who leaves.
  • Roles & Permissions: the owner can fine-tune exactly what each role is allowed to do — these are the workspace's own settings, adjustable any time.
  • Secure invites: invited staff receive a one-time activation link and set their own password — again, no passwords are emailed.
  • Built-in safeguards: only an owner can grant owner-level access, and no-one can accidentally lock themselves out.
What it means: a landlord can safely add an accountant who only touches finances, or maintenance staff who only see maintenance — on their own terms, without involving us.

How your data and accounts are protected now

  • No passwords are ever emailed — everyone sets their own
  • Secure one-time activation links for all new accounts
  • Sign-in guessing blocked by rate-limiting
  • Each landlord's data strictly isolated & tested
  • Staff see and do only what their role permits
  • Landlords control their own team & permissions
  • Public forms protected against spam and bots
  • Over 130 automatic tests run before every update
  • Every change peer-reviewed before going live
  • HTTPS, encrypted identifiers, nightly backups

What's next

Two further hardening steps are planned to complete this programme: securing how uploaded files (photos, receipts) are handled and stored, and final production-environment hardening — including delivering account-activation links by SMS as well as email, so renters and staff without an email address can still be onboarded smoothly. These will be scheduled next.
